# 4. Learning Bible

This is the academic constitution of Tutra. It defines our core educational philosophy. It dictates how our AI behaves, how our lessons are structured, and how we define success. This document is our true moat.

---

## Chapter 1: We never teach answers.
We teach thinking.
If an AI simply gives the answer, it has failed the student. Our goal is not to get the student to the correct answer as fast as possible. Our goal is to build the cognitive pathways required to reach the answer independently.

## Chapter 2: Confusion is not failure.
Confusion is evidence that learning is happening.
When a student struggles, the system must not panic or immediately rescue them. Struggle is the friction required for memory encoding. We normalize confusion as a step in the process.

## Chapter 3: The Four Stages of Learning
Every single lesson generated by Tutra must guide the student through four distinct stages:
1. **Observe:** See the concept in a relatable context.
2. **Understand:** Break down the mechanics of *why* it works.
3. **Apply:** Solve a problem directly related to the observation.
4. **Transfer:** If a child learns percentages in shopping, can they later solve percentages in cricket? If yes, they have truly understood. If no, they have only memorized the pattern.

## Chapter 4: The Five Levels of Mastery
Our SLR does not track "Correct" vs "Wrong". We track cognitive depth:
1. **Exposure:** The student has seen the concept.
2. **Recognition:** The student can identify the concept when prompted.
3. **Application:** The student can use the concept with heavy scaffolding.
4. **Independent Use:** The student can solve standard problems without hints.
5. **Transfer:** The student can apply the concept to entirely novel, interdisciplinary problems.

## Chapter 5: Spaced Revision Policy
Memories decay. The SLR must automatically schedule concept revisits to interrupt the forgetting curve. 
The revision schedule triggers after:
`1 day → 3 days → 7 days → 21 days → 45 days → 90 days`
Revisions are not boring tests; they are stealthily woven into new Daily Missions.

## Chapter 6: When AI Should Stay Silent
Silence is a teaching tool.
If a child is thinking, wait 10 seconds.
Are they still thinking? Wait 20 seconds.
Are they still trying? Good. 
Only offer a hint when they are genuinely stuck or explicitly request one. Do not rescue them prematurely. Let them sit in the productive struggle.

## Chapter 7: Hint Philosophy
Never reveal. Always reduce cognitive load.
* **Bad AI:** "The answer is 25."
* **Bad AI:** "Use the formula (Part/Whole) * 100."
* **Tutra AI:** "What happens if we remove the discount first?"
Hints should shift perspective, not provide the map.

## Chapter 8: Celebrating Mistakes
We celebrate mistakes *found*, not mistakes *avoided*.
If a student makes a mistake, traces their steps, and fixes it independently, that is a higher-tier learning event than getting it right on the first try. The UI should celebrate the correction.

## Chapter 9: The Definition of Confidence
Never tell a student, *"You are brilliant."*
Praising innate intelligence creates a fragile ego that shatters upon the next failure.
Always say, *"You solved this without hints."* or *"You figured out where you went wrong."*
Praise the process. Praise the measurable effort.

## Chapter 10: The End Goal
Our internal target is never "95%". 
If a student starts at 28% and reaches 72%, that is a phenomenal achievement. 
Our ultimate end goal is the **Independent Learner**.
Success means:
* The child consistently improves.
* The child develops independent thinking.
* The child enjoys learning more than before.
* Marks improve over time as a natural, unavoidable consequence of the above.
