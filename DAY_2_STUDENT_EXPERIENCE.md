# Day 2: The Student Experience

**Goal:** Answer the question: *What makes a child voluntarily come back tomorrow?*
**Context:** Sidhiq (Class 8). Yesterday, he completed the Academic Scan. Tutra knows he is working at a Class 7.2 level, strong in Decimals, but struggling with Ratio & Proportion.

---

## 6:30 PM: The Hook
*Sidhiq opens the Tutra app on his mother's phone.*

**Screen 1: The Welcome**
* No dashboards. No "Syllabus Progress". No "Pending Tasks".
* The screen is clean, dark mode, with a single glowing card.

**Tutra:**
"Hey Sidhiq. 🏏"
"CSK needs 42 runs in the last 3 overs."
"Think they can do it?"

**Buttons:**
`[Yes, easy! 🔥]`
`[Tough, but maybe 🤔]`

---

## 6:31 PM: The Setup (Micro-Learning)
*Sidhiq taps `[Yes, easy! 🔥]`.*

**Screen 2: The Context**
* Smooth transition. A quick visual of a cricket scoreboard.

**Tutra:**
"Let's see. If Dhoni hits 3 sixes and 4 fours in those 3 overs..."
"How many runs is that just from boundaries?"

**Input:**
`[ Numeric Keypad appears ]`

---

## 6:32 PM: The First Interaction
*Sidhiq types `[ 34 ]`.*

**Screen 3: Immediate Reaction**
* The number glows green. Haptic vibration.

**Tutra:**
"Boom. 34 runs."
"That leaves exactly 8 runs to score from singles and doubles."
"You calculated that in 4 seconds. Faster than the third umpire."

---

## 6:33 PM: The Actual Lesson (Stealth Mode)
*Tutra seamlessly pivots from the cricket hook into the actual learning gap (Ratio).*

**Screen 4: The Challenge**
**Tutra:**
"Here’s a real challenge though."
"If Dhoni and Jadeja are running between the wickets, and for every 2 runs Dhoni takes, Jadeja takes 3..."
"What is the ratio of their runs?"

**Buttons:**
`[ 2:3 ]`
`[ 3:2 ]`
`[ 5:1 ]`

*Sidhiq taps `[ 2:3 ]`.*

**Tutra:**
"Spot on. 2:3."

---

## 6:34 PM: Stretching the Concept (Adaptive Challenge)
**Screen 5: The Escalation**
**Tutra:**
"Okay, smart guy. If they scored 15 runs together using that exact 2:3 ratio..."
"How many runs did Dhoni score?"

*(This is the actual Class 7 Ratio & Proportion concept he struggled with yesterday.)*

**Input:**
`[ Numeric Keypad ]`  
*(Wait 10 seconds. Sidhiq hasn't answered. He's thinking.)*

**Tutra (Auto-Hint slide-in):**
"Hint: Together, their ratio shares equal 5 parts (2 + 3). 15 runs divided by 5 parts..."

*Sidhiq types `[ 6 ]`.*

---

## 6:35 PM: The Payoff
**Screen 6: The Reward**
* Screen flashes a satisfying animation.

**Tutra:**
"Nailed it."
"Dhoni scored 6, Jadeja scored 9. Total 15."
"You just solved a Class 8 Ratio problem without breaking a sweat."

---

## 6:36 PM: The Pivot to Choice
**Screen 7: Autonomy**
**Tutra:**
"We're on a streak. What's next?"

**Options:**
`[ Give me a harder cricket puzzle ]`
`[ I want to build a space station (Geometry) ]`
`[ I'm done for today ]`

*Sidhiq taps `[ Give me a harder cricket puzzle ]`.*

---

## 6:36 PM – 6:39 PM: The Flow State
*Sidhiq plays 3 more rapid-fire puzzles. They get progressively harder. He gets one wrong, Tutra gives a gentle nudge, he fixes it.*

---

## 6:40 PM: The Cool Down
**Screen 8: Natural Stop**
**Tutra:**
"Alright, Dhoni. That’s 10 solid minutes of brain workout."
"You leveled up your Ratio skills today."

"Come back tomorrow. I have a puzzle about your pocket money that you won't be able to solve."

**Buttons:**
`[ Bet. See you tomorrow. ]`

*App closes.*

---

## Why this works (What makes them come back)
1. **Zero Friction Entry:** No loading screens, no menus, no "Select Subject". It immediately asks a culturally relevant question.
2. **Stealth Assessment:** It taught and tested Ratio & Proportion without ever using the words "Math Class", "Chapter 4", or "Exercise".
3. **Instant Gratification:** Haptic feedback, fast validation ("Boom. 34 runs").
4. **Autonomy:** At the 6-minute mark, the child gets to choose what happens next.
5. **The Open Loop:** The session ends with a cliffhanger challenge for tomorrow ("pocket money puzzle you won't be able to solve").
